Strategy
Defining the right AI starting point
Many organizations recognize the potential of AI but face a complex landscape of options, from off-the-shelf platforms to custom-built systems. A clear framework for evaluating use cases, assessing feasibility, and aligning investments with business priorities can make the difference between momentum and stalled initiatives.
Indicators
AI initiatives distributed across departments without central coordination
Difficulty prioritizing which use cases to pursue first
Uncertainty around build, buy, or fine-tune decisions
We work alongside your team to map the landscape, evaluate options against your specific context, and develop a prioritized roadmap that reflects both technical feasibility and business value.

Engineering
Moving from prototype to production
The transition from a working prototype to a reliable production system is one of the most underestimated challenges in AI adoption. Performance on real-world data, infrastructure readiness, and operational maturity all come into play, and each requires a different set of expertise.
Indicators
Model performance changes significantly between test and production data
Limited infrastructure for deployment, versioning, or monitoring
Data quality and governance gaps surfacing at scale
We assess readiness across data, infrastructure, and process, then work with your engineering team to design a realistic path to production, addressing the gaps that matter most.

Assurance
Evaluating AI systems already in production
Organizations that have deployed AI systems often need independent evaluation, whether to satisfy internal governance requirements, prepare for regulatory obligations, or gain confidence in system behavior around data protection, fairness, and accuracy.
Indicators
Deployed AI systems have not undergone a formal risk or quality assessment
Internal teams would benefit from independent technical validation
No systematic monitoring for drift, bias, or data leakage in place
We provide independent technical evaluations, including privacy risk assessments, adversarial testing, and fairness audits, and deliver clear, evidence-based reports suitable for internal decision-making or regulatory communication.

Frontier
Navigating agentic AI and emerging regulation
Agentic AI systems, those that make autonomous decisions, use tools, and access data across multiple sources, introduce a new category of risk. Existing compliance and governance frameworks were not designed for emergent behavior, and regulation is evolving rapidly to address this.
Indicators
Deploying or evaluating tool-using or multi-agent systems
Open questions around provider responsibility under the EU AI Act
No established risk model for autonomous agent behavior
We help you map the risk landscape for agentic systems, from data flow analysis and supply chain evaluation to regulatory classification, so you can make informed decisions about deployment.

Background
Where we come from

Before founding Modal Resonance, we built and led an AI company focused on synthetic data and privacy-enhancing technologies, serving enterprise clients in regulated industries. The company was acquired by a leading data protection provider, where we continued to lead research and engineering.

During that time, we developed Anonymeter, an open-source privacy risk measurement tool that was evaluated by the French data protection authority (CNIL) and published in a peer-reviewed scientific journal. It is now used by enterprises across Europe.

Our work has included contributions to NIST's Collaborative Research Cycle on privacy risk assessments, IEEE standardization of synthetic data evaluations, collaboration with NVIDIA on privacy-preserving methods, and published research on LLM accuracy evaluation and data anonymization. We have been named as inventors on pending patents in privacy-enhancing technologies (Omar, Matteo).
